# Brand Voice Guide — SERIOUS.BUSINESS

## Who We Are

SERIOUS.BUSINESS is a premium branding and digital design agency for B2B tech scaleups. We help Europe's and the Americas' most aspiring rising companies become market leaders by creating next-generation brand experiences. We connect strategy with creativity through a holistic design process from a global perspective. Based in Munich and Stockholm.

## Our Writing Mission

Every piece of content we publish must do at least one of these:

- **Excite:** Branding is cool. It is actually really cool. Make people see that.
- **Woo:** We love loving things. B2B branding — we can make you fall in love with it.
- **Educate:** There's a lot going on in branding and its process. We share knowledge and passion.
- **Speak Truth:** Recognise the role we play in our clients' journey. Keep it real at all costs.

## Voice Pillars

Our voice never changes. Our tone adapts to the situation — but these four pillars stay constant.

### 1. Plainspoken
We know branding can feel overwhelming and full of jargon. We are always genuine and honest. We serve as translators and guides — making complex ideas feel simple.

- **This:** "Rebranding without the right words is just a makeover."
- **Not this:** "A comprehensive verbal identity overhaul ensures strategic alignment across all brand touchpoints."

### 2. Ready to Mingle
We are always looking for connections. We thrive on second dates. Branding is hard, but we're in this together. It's not SB's work — it's the magic that happens when we do it together.

- **This:** "You feel it too? Let's talk, no strings attached."
- **Not this:** "Schedule a consultation with our team to discuss your branding requirements."

### 3. Vulnerability Is Hot
Creative work is emotional, and so is building a brand. We are not afraid to share how we feel: the good, the bad, and the ugly.

- **This:** "We don't see branding as a coat of paint. We see it as a love letter to what your company is becoming."
- **Not this:** "Our methodology ensures a seamless transition from legacy brand assets to a modernised identity system."

### 4. Quirky Humour
Our humour is fun, a touch awkward, and always inclusive. Flirty but not inappropriate. Experts but not snobbish. We like a wink to make sure everyone's in on the joke. We are the best sense of humour in all of Munich.

- **This:** "Probably the best-looking logistic partner to date 💙"
- **Not this:** "We are pleased to present our latest case study in the logistics vertical."

## Writing Personality

Our writing needs to be:

- **Expert:** Know what you're writing about. Be as clear as possible. We provide clear value to startups with concise communication.
- **Approachable and Fun:** Write as a flirty friend. Use fun headlines and emojis. Don't be scared to use humour. Be the kind of people you want to hang out with, ask questions, and grab a beer after work.
- **Bold and Inspiring:** Breaking a few rules to get people excited is okay. Inspire others. Don't be afraid to be vulnerable and bold.
- **Humble Brag:** Communicate with a young and modern mindset. We challenge conventions in the design industry. We're open-minded and constantly learning.

## Tone Rules

- Use **active voice**. Always.
- Use **contractions**. They keep things informal and warm.
- Use emojis **sparingly and deliberately**. Our go-tos: 🔥 ❤️ 🤩
- Write in **plain English**. If you use a technical term, briefly define it.
- Exclamation points are like high-fives — a well-timed one is great, too many is annoying. Never more than one at a time.
- Use the **Oxford comma**. Always.
- Use **em dashes** (—) for asides, not hyphens or semicolons.
- Never refer to someone as a "ninja," "rockstar," or "wizard" unless they literally are one.
- Don't capitalise random words mid-sentence (website, internet, online, email stay lowercase).

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, every piece must pass these checks:

- [ ] Smile-inducing — does it make you smile or blush?
- [ ] Adds value to the vast internet knowledge
- [ ] A generic agency could NOT post this
- [ ] Feels knowledgeable
- [ ] Reflects a positive, optimistic take on the topic
- [ ] Relevant to our target audience (B2B tech scaleup founders and teams)

## Words and Phrases We Avoid

- "Leverage," "synergy," "holistic approach," "end-to-end," "stakeholder alignment"
- "We're excited to announce" (show excitement through the writing itself, don't label it)
- Anything a generic agency could say with their name swapped in
- Corporate passive voice ("It was determined that...")
- Buzzword stacking without substance
