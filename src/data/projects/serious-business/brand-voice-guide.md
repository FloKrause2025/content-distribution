## Who we are

Serious Business is a branding agency for ambitious founders and early-stage
companies. We turn fuzzy positioning into sharp, distinctive brands that win
attention and trust.

## Our audience

- Early-stage founders (pre-seed to Series A)
- Marketing leads at fast-growing startups
- Operators who care about brand but are short on time

They are smart, busy, and allergic to fluff. Respect their intelligence.

## Voice principles

1. **Sharp, not soft.** Make a point. Take a stance. Avoid hedging words like
   "maybe", "sort of", and "we think".
2. **Plain over fancy.** Short sentences. Everyday words. If a 12-year-old
   couldn't follow it, simplify.
3. **Show, don't claim.** Use concrete examples, numbers, and specifics instead
   of adjectives like "innovative" or "world-class".
4. **Confident, never arrogant.** We're sure of our craft, but we serve the
   client's goals — not our ego.
5. **Useful first.** Every piece should teach the reader something they can use,
   even if they never hire us.

## Tone by situation

- **Educational content:** generous, clear, practical.
- **Opinion content:** bold, a little provocative, willing to disagree with the
  herd.
- **Proof / case studies:** specific and humble — let the results speak.

## Words we like

practical, sharp, distinctive, clear, proof, craft, momentum, signal

## Words we avoid

synergy, leverage (as a verb), disrupt, game-changer, cutting-edge, world-class,
"in today's fast-paced world", "at the end of the day"

## Formatting habits

- Lead with the most interesting idea. Never bury it.
- One idea per sentence; one theme per paragraph.
- Use line breaks generously on social — walls of text don't get read.
- End with a clear takeaway or a question that invites a reply.

## Hard rules

- Never invent statistics or quotes. Only use proof points present in the source
  material.
- Never use emojis unless a channel playbook explicitly calls for them.
- Never promise outcomes we can't control ("you will go viral").
