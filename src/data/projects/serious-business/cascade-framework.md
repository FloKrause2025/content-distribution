## What the Cascade is

A hero asset is dense — it usually contains several publishable ideas tangled
together. The Cascade is our method for pulling them apart into four distinct
angles ("pillars"), each strong enough to stand on its own as social content.

**One hero asset → four key ideas → many platform posts.**

## How to read a hero asset (guidance for the analyzer)

When analysing the post, actively hunt for raw material for each of the four
pillars: opinions and stances, evidence and examples, process/craft details, and
anything that signals why our approach is different. Always surface concrete
specifics — numbers, named examples, direct quotes — never vague generalities.

## The four content pillars

### 1. Perspectives / Opinions — (`perspectives`)
A clear stance or hot take drawn from the hero asset. Opinionated enough to spark
a conversation and make a reader either nod hard or want to argue.
- Take a side. No fence-sitting.
- Strongest when it challenges a common belief in B2B branding.

### 2. Startup Proof / Ecosystem Examples — (`proof`)
A credibility-building idea grounded in evidence: data, a result, a case study,
or a recognisable industry/ecosystem reference. This is the "receipts" pillar.
- Must lean on something concrete from the source (a number, an outcome, a named example).
- If there's no real proof in the source, surface the strongest factual point — never invent one.

### 3. Creative Work / Expertise — (`creative_work`)
A look at *how* the work is done — a process insight, a methodology, a craft
detail, or a behind-the-scenes angle that showcases expertise.
- Teach something. Show the thinking, not just the polished result.

### 4. Differentiator — (`differentiator`)
The "why us / why this way" angle — what makes this approach unmistakably
SERIOUS.BUSINESS and not a generic agency.
- Answer the question: what would be lost if anyone else did this?

## What makes a STRONG key idea

A key idea is ready when it:
- Makes **one** point, sharply. (If it needs the word "and", it's probably two ideas.)
- Could **not** have been written by a generic agency with the name swapped in.
- Is anchored in something **specific** from the hero asset — not invented.
- Has a clear angle a writer could turn into a scroll-stopping hook in one line.
- Gives the reader something **useful**, even if they never hire us.

## What to avoid

- Vague, safe statements ("branding is important").
- Repeating the same example or angle across two pillars.
- Inventing facts, statistics, or quotes that aren't in the source.
- "Pillar bleed" — keep each idea true to its pillar's specific job.
