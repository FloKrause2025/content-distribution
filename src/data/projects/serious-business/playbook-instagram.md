## Shared voice on Instagram

- Visual-first thinking: every idea should be easy to picture.
- Punchy and skimmable. Short lines. One idea at a time.
- Warmer and a touch more energetic than LinkedIn, but still no fluff.
- Emojis: sparingly allowed here (0–2 per slide/section) to aid scanning. Never
  decorative spam.

---

## Format A — Carousel

A swipeable set of slides (usually 6–9). Each slide is one beat of the idea.

**Structure:**

1. **Slide 1 — Hook slide:** A bold title that makes someone stop and swipe.
   5–8 words max. Optionally a one-line subhead.
2. **Slides 2–N — One point per slide:** A short heading + 1–2 supporting lines.
   Keep each slide to ~20 words so it's readable on a phone.
3. **Second-to-last slide — Payoff:** The key takeaway, stated simply.
4. **Last slide — CTA:** "Save this", "Follow for more", or a question. Include
   the brand handle.

**Output format:** Label each slide clearly, e.g.

```
Slide 1 (Hook): ...
Slide 2: ...
...
Slide 8 (CTA): ...
```

Aim for 6–9 slides total.

---

## Format B — Reel Script

A short-form video script (20–45 seconds spoken). Written to be read aloud.

**Structure:**

1. **Hook (first 3 seconds):** A spoken line that stops the scroll. Pair it with
   an on-screen text suggestion in [brackets].
2. **Setup:** One or two lines of context.
3. **Value:** The main points, delivered fast. Keep sentences speakable.
4. **CTA:** Tell the viewer what to do next (follow, save, comment).

**Output format:** Provide the spoken script plus on-screen text cues, e.g.

```
[On-screen: "Most rebrands fail"]
VO: Here's why most rebrands quietly fail...

[On-screen: "It's not the logo"]
VO: ...
```

Keep the whole script to roughly 80–120 words so it fits in under 45 seconds.

---

## Do

- Front-load the value; assume people leave after 2 seconds.
- Use concrete examples and numbers from the source material.
- Keep every line phone-readable.

## Don't

- Don't write paragraphs — this is a visual/spoken medium.
- Don't overuse emojis or hashtags.
- Don't bury the hook.
