# Channel Playbook — Instagram (Carousel + Reels)

## Platform
Instagram (@seriousbusiness.agency)

## Audience on This Channel
Design-aware founders, creative directors, branding peers, junior designers who aspire to agency-level work, and potential clients who follow for visual inspiration before they ever reach out. They're scrolling casually — between apps, on the couch, on a break. Content needs to stop the thumb in under 1.5 seconds.

## Format Specs

### Carousels (Primary format)
- **Slides:** 5-8 slides. Never fewer than 5, never more than 10.
- **Slide 1 (Cover):** This IS the hook. Bold statement, curiosity gap, or provocative question. Minimal text — max 8 words. Must work as a standalone thumbnail in the grid.
- **Slides 2-7 (Body):** One idea per slide. Max 25 words per slide. Use visual hierarchy — headline + supporting line. No walls of text.
- **Final Slide (CTA):** Save, share, follow, or "DM us [keyword]." Keep it simple.
- **Design:** Clean, premium, on-brand. White space matters. Never cluttered.

### Reels (Secondary format)
- **Length:** 15-45 seconds max. Shorter is better.
- **Hook:** First 1.5 seconds must create a curiosity gap or pattern interrupt.
- **Format:** Talking head, screen recording of work, before/after reveal, timelapse of design process.
- **Captions:** Always on. Most people watch on mute.
- **Style:** Simple, not overproduced. Authenticity beats polish on this platform.

## Tone on This Channel
More visual, more playful, more punchy than LinkedIn. The agency speaks here — not an individual founder. Confident but never arrogant. Educational but never lecturing. The vibe is "cool creative friend who happens to be ridiculously good at branding."

Short sentences. Fragments are fine. Personality over formality.

## Hook Rules (Carousel Cover / Reel Opening)

**Cover slide patterns that work:**
- Curiosity gap: "The branding mistake that cost this startup €2M"
- Bold statement: "Your brand isn't your logo"
- Before/after tease: "Swipe to see the transformation →"
- Direct call-out: "Series A founders: stop doing this"
- Simple question: "Would you trust this brand?"

**What doesn't work:**
- "5 tips for better branding" (generic, no curiosity gap)
- Too much text on the cover slide
- Stock imagery
- Anything that looks like every other design agency's carousel

## Caption Style
- **Length:** 50-150 words. Front-load the value — most people won't tap "more."
- **First line:** Reinforce the carousel hook or add context. Not a repeat of slide 1.
- **Body:** 2-3 sentences max expanding on the core insight.
- **CTA:** One clear action — save, share, comment, or DM.
- **Hashtags:** 5-10 relevant hashtags. Mix of broad (#branding, #startup) and niche (#b2bbranding, #scaleuplife). Place below a line break.
- **Emojis:** 1-3 max. Use as punctuation, not decoration.

## Content Themes That Work

1. **Before/After Reveals** — Show the transformation. Create a curiosity gap about the end result. The "swipe to reveal" mechanic is native to Instagram.
2. **Process Breakdowns** — How we approached a specific problem. Not the full case study — one interesting decision or moment.
3. **Hot Takes** — Opinionated design/branding takes that start conversations in comments.
4. **Client Work Showcases** — Tied to an idea, not "just another client." The work illustrates a point.
5. **Quick Tips** — Actionable, specific, visual. "Do this, not that" format.

## Example Carousel

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
"Your scaleup brand looks like everyone else's"

**Slide 2:**
"Same dark mode. Same gradient. Same geometric sans-serif. Same 'we empower teams to...' copy."

**Slide 3:**
"When every B2B tech brand looks the same, none of them are memorable."

**Slide 4:**
"The fix isn't being 'more creative.' It's being more honest about who you actually are."

**Slide 5:**
"We worked with a logistics scaleup. Everyone expected dark, techy, serious. They chose otters. 🦦"

**Slide 6:**
"The result? A brand people actually remember. And talk about. And smile at."

**Slide 7 (CTA):**
"What's the most forgettable B2B brand you've seen? Tell us below 👇"

**Caption:**
Every B2B tech brand deck we've seen in the last year looks eerily similar. Dark backgrounds, neon accents, vague empowerment language. It's safe. And safe is invisible.

The scaleups that win aren't the ones with the biggest ad budget — they're the ones brave enough to look like themselves.

## Example Reel

**Hook (0-2 sec):** "We rebranded 180 scaleups. This is what 90% of them get wrong."

**Body (2-25 sec):** "They come to us wanting a new logo. A fresh website. New colours. But when we ask 'what's your story?' — silence. You can't design a brand if you can't say what you stand for. The visuals come last. The story comes first."

**End (25-30 sec):** "If you can't explain your brand in one sentence, you don't have a brand yet. You have a logo."

**Caption:**
It's not a design problem. It's a clarity problem. The visuals are the easy part — figuring out what to say is where the real work happens.

Tag a founder who needs to hear this 😅
