# Channel Playbook — LinkedIn Founder Post

> **Starter content.** Replace with Serious Business's real LinkedIn playbook.
> Editable from the Knowledge Base page.

## Goal

A first-person post written as if from the founder. It builds authority and
sparks conversation in the feed of other founders and operators.

## Format & structure

1. **Hook (line 1):** One punchy line that stops the scroll. A bold claim, a
   surprising number, or a contrarian take. No throat-clearing.
2. **Line break.** Always put a blank line after the hook so LinkedIn shows it
   as the preview.
3. **Context (2-4 short lines):** Set up the idea. One sentence per line.
4. **The body (the meat):** Make the argument. Use short paragraphs, line
   breaks, and the occasional single-line punch. A simple list is welcome when
   it adds clarity.
5. **Takeaway:** The one thing the reader should remember.
6. **CTA:** A genuine question that invites replies, or a soft prompt to share.

## Length

- 800–1,500 characters is the sweet spot.
- Plenty of white space. Never more than 2–3 sentences in a block.

## Voice on this channel

- First person ("I", "we").
- Conversational and direct. Like talking to one smart friend.
- Opinionated. It's fine to disagree with conventional wisdom.
- No hashtags in the body; at most 1–3 relevant ones at the very end.
- No emojis (per brand guide).

## Do

- Open with the single most interesting idea.
- Tell a small, specific story or use a concrete example.
- Use "you" to speak directly to the reader.

## Don't

- Don't start with "I'm excited to share…" or "Thoughts?".
- Don't write a wall of text.
- Don't stuff it with buzzwords.

## Example (structure to imitate, not copy)

> Most rebrands fail for a boring reason.
>
> It's not the logo. It's not the colors.
>
> It's that nobody decided what the company actually stands for first.
>
> We've watched six-figure rebrands collapse because the team skipped the one
> unglamorous step: writing down a single, sharp positioning statement everyone
> agrees on.
>
> Get that right and the visuals almost design themselves.
> Get it wrong and you're just repainting a house with no foundation.
>
> If you're planning a rebrand this year, start with one sentence: "We're the
> only ___ that ___."
