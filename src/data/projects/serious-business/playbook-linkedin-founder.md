# Channel Playbook — LinkedIn Founder Post

## Platform
LinkedIn (personal founder profile)

## Audience on This Channel
B2B tech startup founders, CTOs, VPs of Marketing at Series A/B scaleups, investors, and branding/design peers. They scroll LinkedIn during work hours, between meetings. They engage with content that feels like a smart friend sharing a hard-won lesson — not a brand broadcasting.

## Format Specs
- **Length:** 800-1300 characters (sweet spot for engagement without "see more" fatigue)
- **Structure:** Hook → Story/Insight → Proof/Example → Reframe → CTA or open question
- **Line breaks:** Use single-line paragraphs. One thought per line. White space is your friend.
- **No hashtags** in the body. 3-5 hashtags max at the very end, separated by a line break.
- **No links in the post body.** If linking, put it in the first comment.
- **Emojis:** Use 0-2 max. Never as bullet points. Only for emphasis or personality.

## Tone on This Channel
More personal than the SB website. This is a founder talking, not the agency. First person ("I" not "we"). Vulnerable, opinionated, conversational. It should read like a voice note transcribed — not a press release polished.

## Hook Rules
The first 2 lines decide everything. They must create tension, curiosity, or a pattern interrupt.

**Hook patterns that work:**
- Contrarian take: "Most scaleups don't have a branding problem. They have a confusion problem."
- Personal confession: "We lost a pitch last month. And honestly? We deserved to."
- Specific number: "180 scaleups. 10 years. Here's the one mistake I see every single time."
- Bold claim: "Your website is costing you more than your worst sales hire."
- Question: "When was the last time a B2B brand made you feel something?"

**Hooks that DON'T work:**
- "I'm excited to share..." (instant scroll-past)
- "In today's fast-paced business environment..." (corporate zombie)
- Starting with a hashtag or emoji
- Anything that could be the opening line of a LinkedIn automation sequence

## CTA Style
End with one of:
- An open question that invites genuine comments (not "agree?")
- A soft pointer ("DM me if this hit close to home")
- A reframe that leaves them thinking (no CTA needed if the insight is strong enough)

Never: "Like and share if you agree!" or "Follow me for more content like this."

## Example Posts

**Example 1 — Perspectives/Opinion:**
```
Most scaleups don't have a branding problem.

They have a confusion problem.

Their product is solid. Their team is sharp. But their website says one thing, their pitch deck says another, and their LinkedIn says something else entirely.

I call it the confusion tax — the invisible cost of every prospect who bounced because they couldn't figure out what you actually do in 10 seconds.

More outbound calls won't fix this.
A new logo won't fix this.
Another "we're excited to announce" post definitely won't fix this.

Clarity will.

What's the most confusing B2B website you've seen lately? (Don't say yours 😅)

#branding #b2bmarketing #startups #scaleups
```

**Example 2 — Vulnerability/Behind the Scenes:**
```
We got the brand strategy completely wrong on the first round.

The client was kind about it. We weren't kind to ourselves.

We went back to the research. Re-interviewed their customers. Sat with the discomfort of having missed something obvious.

Round two was the best work we've done all year.

The lesson isn't "fail forward" or whatever LinkedIn loves to say. The lesson is: the first version is supposed to be wrong. That's not failure — that's the process working.

If your agency hands you a "final" brand on the first pass, be worried.

#branding #agencylife #b2bbranding
```

**Example 3 — Proof/Results:**
```
180 scaleups branded over 10 years.

Here's the pattern nobody talks about:

The ones that grew fastest after rebranding weren't the ones with the prettiest logo.

They were the ones who finally had a story their sales team could tell in one sentence.

Branding isn't decoration. It's internal alignment made visible.

One of our clients went from a 45-second elevator pitch to 8 words. Their close rate went up 30% in the next quarter.

Not because the words were magic. Because everyone was finally saying the same thing.

#branding #b2bsales #startupgrowth #scaleups
```
